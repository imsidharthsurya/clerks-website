Officer Newman

Officer Brad Newman got a kickoff to his law enforcement career when he served in the Military Police Corps. When settleing back into civilian life getting a job with law enforcement seemed only a natural fit for Brad. He has worked at the Terre Haute Police Department since 2005 and has loved his postition there.

Clerk Newman

When Brad started his role as Clerk his main goal was to bring the office into the 21st Century. Since taking office he has helped transition the court system from a paper system to an e-file system making the courts run more efficient and enviromentally friendly. He has also helped bring credit card and debit cards as payment methods in the county.

As Clerk one of Brad's main jobs is to ensure elections are run as smooth and that as many people as possible are enfranchised. Brad has has fulfilled these duties by taking modern, innovative leaps in the Clerk's office. His accomplishments include visiting Terre Haute North, South, and West Vigo High School with voting machines to teach kids to vote, setting up a voting center in Honey Creek Mall, and making voting accessible to college students with a voting center in Indiana State University.

Under Brad's great leadership voting in Vigo County saw unprecedented turnouts in 2016 and 2018. He ran media blitzes and operated over twenty polling locations in Vigo County. The best part is, even with record turnouts, the election budget has decreased since Brad has taken office!

Citizen Newman

Brad Newman is actively inlolved in his community trying to make a difference. He is involved with numerous non-profits throughout the community. These include the Fraternal Order of Police, the Wabash Valley Community Foundation 100 Men That Give, and Soggy Donuts.

Soggy Donuts is an organization that Brad helped found after the flooding of 2008. Him and a group of officers realized that emergency services personnel of Vigo County needed help cleaning up after the flood and moving "soggy" items. With this in mind and embracing the dount sterotypes, Soggy Donuts was formed! The Soggy Donut Fund is still in effect today and it still aims to delvier assistance to emergency services personnel when disaster strikes. Brad Newman currently sits as President of this organization.

When not volunteering or working Brad has many hobbies. He loves watching sports and spending time with his wife and son.
